# Isabelle's Gluten-Free Recipe System
## Design Documentation & User Journey

---

## Persona

**Name:** Isabelle
**Age:** 20
**Condition:** Celiac Disease
**Primary Device:** Laptop (MacBook)
**Secondary Device:** iPhone (for shopping)
**Mindset:** Confident, adventurous, joyful

Isabelle has been gluten-free for years and has completely embraced it. She doesn't see celiac disease as a restriction - it's just part of who she is. She loves discovering new recipes, trying different cuisines, and cooking beautiful meals. The system is her daily companion for culinary inspiration and confident shopping.

### Scene 4: Recipe Details
**Time:** 7:18 AM
**Location:** Reading through the recipe, planning ahead

The recipe expands into a detailed view with a large hero image at the top:

**Header:**
- Stunning photo of green curry in a white bowl
- "Green Curry Shrimp"
- 30 minutes • Medium • Serves 2

**Description:**
"Aromatic Thai green curry with succulent shrimp, bamboo shoots, and Thai basil in creamy coconut milk. Perfectly balanced spice and naturally gluten-free."

**Equipment Needed:**
- Large wok or deep skillet
- Rice cooker or medium saucepan with lid
- Chef's knife
- Cutting board
- Measuring spoons
- Measuring cups
- Mixing bowls (2)
- Wooden spoon or spatula

At the top, right below the recipe name, there's a prominent **serving size selector** showing "How many servings?" with options: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

Currently it's set to "2" (the recipe default). Below that:

**Ingredients:**
- Large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- Coconut milk
- Gluten-free green curry paste
- Bamboo shoots
- Red bell pepper
- Fresh Thai basil
- Gluten-free fish sauce
- Coconut sugar
- Jasmine rice for serving

The ingredients are listed, but no amounts are shown yet. The instructions are also waiting for her to select a serving size.

She wants to make extra for tomorrow's lunch, so she clicks the selector and chooses **4 servings**.

**What happens:**
- The page makes a quick call to the server
- Within a second, amounts appear next to every ingredient:

**Ingredients:** (for 4 servings)
- 2 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cans (14 oz) coconut milk
- 4 tbsp gluten-free green curry paste
- 2 cups bamboo shoots
- 2 red bell peppers
- Fresh Thai basil
- 4 tbsp gluten-free fish sauce
- 2 tbsp coconut sugar
- Jasmine rice for serving

**Mise en Place (Prep First):**
1. Start jasmine rice in rice cooker or saucepan (enough for 4 servings)
2. Peel and devein 2 lbs shrimp if not already done, pat dry with paper towels
3. Slice 2 red bell peppers into thin strips
4. Drain 2 cups bamboo shoots
5. Pick Thai basil leaves from stems (about 1 cup)
6. Measure out 4 tbsp curry paste, 4 tbsp fish sauce, and 2 tbsp coconut sugar into small bowls
7. Shake coconut milk cans well

*Clean: Wash cutting board and knife. Wipe down counter.*

**Cooking:**
1. Heat wok over medium-high heat, add 1 can coconut milk
2. Stir constantly until coconut milk begins to separate and oil appears (2-3 min)
3. Add 4 tbsp curry paste, stir for 1 minute until fragrant and well combined
4. Add remaining can of coconut milk, stir well
5. Bring to a gentle simmer, reduce heat to medium

*Clean: Rinse the empty coconut milk cans and measuring spoons.*

6. Add 2 cups bamboo shoots and sliced red bell peppers, simmer 2 minutes
7. Add 2 lbs shrimp, cook 5-7 minutes until pink and cooked through
8. Stir in 4 tbsp fish sauce and 2 tbsp coconut sugar, taste and adjust
9. Remove from heat, stir in Thai basil leaves
10. Let rest 1 minute while basil wilts

**Plating:**
11. Fluff rice and divide into 4 bowls
12. Ladle curry over rice, garnish with extra basil if desired

*Clean: Turn off heat. Soak wok in warm soapy water while eating.*

Perfect! All the amounts are now calculated for 4 servings.

A prominent button appears: **"Send Shopping List to iPhone"**

She clicks it.

## The System: Two Apps Working Together

**Web App (Laptop/Desktop)**
Isabelle uses this for discovering recipes, selecting what to make, and cooking. It's her creative space where she explores cuisines and plans meals.

**iOS App (iPhone)**
Her shopping companion at the store. Receives lists from the web app, helps her find ingredients or substitutes, and syncs back what she actually bought.

---

## Key Features & User Experience

### Web Application Features

#### 1. Visual Protein Selection
- Full-screen grid of high-quality protein images
- 8-12 options (Shrimp, Chicken, Salmon, Beef, Pork, Tofu, Eggs, Lamb, Duck, etc.)
- Smooth transitions between screens

#### 2. Cuisine Selection
- Grid of cuisine imagery (not flags, but food/ingredient compositions)
- 10-15 cuisines (Thai, Italian, Mexican, Japanese, Indian, Mediterranean, Chinese, Korean, Vietnamese, French, Spanish, Middle Eastern, American, etc.)
- Each image evokes the essence of the cuisine

#### 3. Recipe Discovery with Smart Filtering
- Recipe cards in scrollable grid (3-4 columns on desktop)
- Each card shows: Photo, Name, Time, Difficulty
- **Smart Secondary Ingredient Wheel** at bottom
  - Horizontal scrollable row
  - Shows only ingredients common to the selected protein + cuisine combo
  - Example: Thai + Shrimp = Coconut, Lime, Basil, Lemongrass, Peanuts, Mango
  - Example: Italian + Chicken = Tomato, Garlic, Basil, Parmesan, Lemon
- Click ingredient to filter
- Non-matching recipes fade out smoothly

#### 4. Recipe Detail Screen
- Hero image at top
- Recipe name, time, difficulty
- **Prominent serving size selector** at top ("How many servings?" - dropdown or stepper)
- Description paragraph
- **Equipment needed list** (organized by category)
- **Ingredients list** (organized by category)
  - Initially shows ingredient names ONLY (no amounts)
  - After user selects serving size → server calculates amounts → amounts appear instantly
- **Mise en Place instructions** (prep all ingredients first)
  - Initially shows steps without specific quantities
  - After serving selection → quantities appear throughout
  - Includes "Clean as you go" notes in italics after relevant steps
- **Cooking instructions** (actual cooking steps)
  - Initially without quantities, updated after serving selection
  - Includes "Clean as you go" notes integrated throughout
- **Plating instructions** (final presentation)
- **"Send Shopping List to iPhone"** button (only enabled after serving selection)

#### 5. Substitution Awareness
- Receives updates when user makes substitutions in iOS app
- Shows notification badge: "Shopping list updated"
- Displays substitutions panel showing what was swapped
- Recipe stays accurate to what user actually bought

### iOS Application Features

#### 1. Shopping List Receiver
- Push notification when new list arrives from web app
- Opens to organized shopping list
- Shows recipe name and serving size at top
- Organized by category (Proteins, Produce, Pantry, Dairy, etc.)
- Checkbox UI for each item

#### 2. Item Detail & Substitutions
- Tap any item to see details
- Shows recommended brands
- Lists trusted gluten-free alternatives
- Provides substitution options with instructions
- Pre-programmed guidance (no AI generation)

#### 3. Substitution Tracking
- User can select which substitution they made
- "Use [Alternative Brand]" button
- "Make my own" button
- "Use [Alternative Ingredient]" button
- Selection is saved and item is checked off

#### 4. Sync Back to Web App
- Substitutions automatically sync back
- Web app receives real-time updates
- User sees accurate ingredient list when cooking

#### 5. Simple, Clean Interface
- Native iOS design
- Focus on usability in a store environment
- Large, tappable items
- Quick access to substitution help
- Offline-capable (list is cached locally)

## Design Philosophy

This system is built around a few core principles that make Isabelle's life joyful:

### 1. Intentional Serving Selection

**The Experience:**
When Isabelle opens a recipe, she sees ingredients WITHOUT amounts. There's a prominent "How many servings?" selector at the top. Once she chooses (say, 4 servings), amounts appear instantly throughout the recipe - in ingredients AND instructions.

**Why:**
- No confusing default amounts that might not match her needs
- Forces her to think: "How much am I making?"
- Everything is calculated for her exact needs
- Clean, uncluttered initial view

**In the Story:**
Scene 4 - Isabelle selects 4 servings and watches the recipe populate with precise amounts

---

### 2. Equipment First

**The Experience:**
Every recipe shows all equipment needed before the ingredients list.

**Why:**
- Gather everything before starting
- No surprises mid-cooking ("Oh, I need a food processor!")
- Supports the mise en place philosophy
- Reduces stress and improves success

---

### 3. Mise en Place & Clean-As-You-Go

**The Experience:**
Instructions are broken into three phases:
- **Mise en Place**: Prep everything first (chop, measure, organize)
- **Cooking**: The actual cooking steps
- **Plating**: Final presentation

Each phase includes "clean as you go" notes in italics.

**Why:**
- Professional kitchen approach adapted for home cooking
- Kitchen stays organized throughout
- Especially important for celiac disease (avoid cross-contamination)
- Reduces post-meal cleanup stress
- Makes cooking feel manageable and joyful

**In the Story:**
Scene 7 - Isabelle preps everything first, cooks with confidence, and her kitchen stays clean throughout

---

This approach mirrors professional kitchen practices and makes cooking less stressful for home cooks managing celiac disease. The focus is on joy, confidence, and empowerment - not restriction.
